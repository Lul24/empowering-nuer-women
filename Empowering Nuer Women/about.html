About Empowering Nuer Women

We are a women-led initiative committed to transparency, accountability, and sustainable development in South Sudan.

Our Story

Empowering Nuer Women was founded in 2018 by a group of passionate Nuer women who recognized the urgent need for sustainable development and empowerment programs in their communities. What began as a small community initiative has grown into a recognized humanitarian organization serving thousands of women and families across South Sudan.

Our founders understood that lasting change comes from within communities. That's why we've always prioritized local leadership, cultural sensitivity, and community participation in all our programs. Today, we continue to build on this foundation, expanding our reach and deepening our impact.

We believe that when women are empowered, entire communities thrive. Our work focuses on creating opportunities for women to become agents of change, breaking cycles of poverty and building pathways to sustainable futures.

Our Core Values

The principles that guide everything we do

Integrity

We operate with complete transparency, ensuring every donation is tracked and every impact is measured and reported.

Community-Led

We believe those closest to the challenges are best positioned to create solutions. Local leadership guides our work.

Equality

We champion gender equality and work to ensure all community members have equal opportunities to thrive.

Sustainability

We build programs that create lasting change, focusing on skills, infrastructure, and systems that endure.

Accountability

We are accountable to the communities we serve, our donors, and our partners. Every decision is made with care.

Collaboration

We believe in the power of partnership and work alongside local organizations, government, and international partners.

Our Journey

Milestones in our mission to empower women and communities

2018

Foundation

Empowering Nuer Women is founded by a group of passionate community leaders in Juba, South Sudan.

2019

First Education Program

Launched adult literacy classes reaching 200 women in Bor and Malakal communities.

2020

Emergency Response

Provided emergency food and medical aid to over 500 families during the COVID-19 pandemic.

2021

Healthcare Initiative

Established mobile health clinics serving remote villages with limited healthcare access.

2022

Vocational Training

Launched vocational training programs in tailoring, weaving, and small business management.

2023

Digital Transparency Hub

Launched our digital platform for real-time impact tracking and transparent reporting.

2024

Expansion & Growth

Expanded operations to reach over 5,000 beneficiaries across South Sudan.
